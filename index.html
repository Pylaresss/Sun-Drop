🌱 About the Project

Solar powered irrigation system for a sustainable agriculture

This project presents a smart solar-powered irrigation system designed to improve water management in rural agricultural environments. In regions such as Madagascar, irrigation is often manual and inefficient, leading to significant water losses. According to the FAO, up to 30–60% of irrigation water can be lost due to poor control and system inefficiencies. Our solution aims to address this challenge by combining renewable energy, embedded control, and real-time monitoring.

System overview

The system is built around an ESP32 microcontroller connected to multiple sensors and actuators. It is powered by a photovoltaic panel coupled with a battery storage system, ensuring full autonomy from the electrical grid. Environmental conditions are continuously measured using soil moisture, temperature, and water level sensors. Based on these inputs, the system controls two key actuators: a water pump used to fill a storage tank, and an solenoid valve used to irrigate the field. This architecture ensures a clear separation between water storage and irrigation functions, improving both reliability and energy efficiency.

Smart irrigation decision

The irrigation strategy is based on a decision algorithm that evaluates real-time sensor data and applies predefined thresholds and safety conditions. Irrigation is triggered only when soil moisture is below a critical level, the water tank contains sufficient water, and environmental conditions are suitable. To avoid unnecessary energy consumption and system instability, the pump and the electrovalve are never activated simultaneously. This control logic ensures optimal use of both water and energy resources while preventing over-irrigation and equipment stress.

IoT Monitoring platform

All system data is transmitted to a cloud platform and displayed on a web dashboard, allowing real-time monitoring from any device. Key parameters such as soil moisture, temperature, tank level, system status, and energy consumption are visualized in an intuitive interface. The platform also integrates warning indicators and decision-support tools, enabling the user to quickly identify critical situations such as low battery, low water level, or reduced solar production. This connectivity transforms the system into a smart decision-support tool rather than a simple automated device.

By combining intelligent control, renewable energy and remote monitoring, the system can reduce water consumption by up to 30–50% compared to traditional irrigation methods, while improving reliability and reducing operational costs. It provides a scalable and sustainable solution adapted to smallholder farming conditions.
